Accidental Burn Treatment
Immediate Step-by-Step Instructions:
  1. Cool the burn: Hold the burned area under cool (not cold) running water for at least 10–20 minutes.
  2. Remove jewelry/belts: Do this gently before the area begins to swell.
  3. Do not pop blisters: Fluid-filled blisters protect against infection. If one pops, clean it with mild soap/water.
  4. Apply moisturizer/aloe: Once the burn is cooled, apply a soothing lotion to prevent drying.
  5. Bandage loosely: Use a sterile gauze bandage. Avoid fluffy cotton that can stick.
WARNING: Seek immediate ER care for large burns, face burns, or electrical burns.
Cuts & Bleeding Treatment
Simple Steps to Stop Bleeding:
  1. Wash your hands: Avoid infecting the wound by using soap or gloves.
  2. Apply pressure: Use a clean cloth or bandage and press firmly for several minutes.
  3. Clean the wound: Rinse with clear water once bleeding stops. Clean the area around it with soap.
  4. Apply antibiotic/ointment: Thin layer of Neosporin or similar to keep the surface moist.
  5. Cover it: Use a bandage or gauze to keep the wound clean and bacteria-free.
High Fever Precautions
How to Manage Fever:
  1. Drink plenty of fluids: Fever can cause dehydration. Drink water, juice, or broth.
  2. Rest: Your body needs energy to recover and fight infection.
  3. Stay cool: Dress in lightweight clothing and keep the room temperature comfortable.
  4. Cold compress: Apply a damp cloth to the forehead if the person feels unwell.
  5. OTC Medication: Use acetaminophen or ibuprofen as directed by a pharmacist.
CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
Hands-Only CPR Steps:
  1. Check the scene: Ensure it's safe for you to help.
  2. Check responsiveness: Tap their shoulder and shout. If no response, call 108 immediately.
  3. Chest compressions: Place hands in the center of the chest. Push hard and fast (100-120 bpm).
  4. Rescue breaths: If trained, give 2 breaths after every 30 compressions.
  5. Continue: Do not stop until paramedics arrive or an AED is available.
